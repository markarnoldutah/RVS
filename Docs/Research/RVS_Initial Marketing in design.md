Getting the **first 10 paying RV dealers** is the hardest step, and it determines whether you hit **$50K ARR in 4–6 months**. The key is to **avoid building in isolation** and instead use a **design-partner sales approach**.

Below is the fastest, most realistic path.

---

# Goal

**10 paying dealers within ~90–120 days**

Example math:

| Price   | Customers | MRR    |
| ------- | --------- | ------ |
| $299/mo | 10        | $2,990 |
| $399/mo | 10        | $3,990 |

Add a few more customers and you reach **$4k+ MRR (~$50k ARR)**.

---

# Step 1 — Recruit 5 Design Partners (Weeks 1–4)

Before the product is finished, recruit **5 dealerships willing to test the tool**.

You are not selling yet.
You are asking them to help shape the product.

Message example:

> I’m building a tool that helps RV dealerships reduce service phone calls by letting customers submit service requests online with VIN and photos.
>
> I’m looking for a few dealerships willing to test the early version and give feedback.

Design partners receive:

• free access during development
• influence over features
• early adopter pricing

This dramatically increases your odds of adoption.

---

# Step 2 — Where to Find Dealers

There are roughly **2,000+ RV dealerships in North America**, including independent dealers and multi-location groups.

Focus on **mid-size dealerships first**.

Ideal target:

• 5–15 technicians
• service backlog
• independent ownership

Avoid large corporate chains initially.

---

## Places to find dealers

### Google Maps

Search:

```
RV dealer near me
RV service center
RV repair
```

Most dealerships list:

• website
• phone
• service department email

---

### LinkedIn

Search for:

• Service Manager
• Fixed Operations Manager
• RV Service Director

Example companies include **Camping World**, **General RV Center**, and **Lazydays RV**, but smaller independent dealers usually move faster.

---

### RV Dealer Associations

Many dealers are connected through organizations like the **RV Dealers Association**.

Dealer conferences and online groups are good networking channels.

---

# Step 3 — How to Contact Them

Cold email and LinkedIn messages work surprisingly well in this niche.

Dealers are used to vendors contacting them.

---

## Cold Email Template

Subject:

Reducing service phone calls at RV dealerships

Body:

> Hi [Name],
>
> I’m building a simple tool that lets RV customers submit service requests online with VIN and photos.
>
> The goal is to reduce service phone calls and give technicians better information before the RV arrives.
>
> I’m looking for a few dealerships willing to test the early version and give feedback.
>
> Would you be open to a quick 10-minute demo?

Keep it short.

---

# Step 4 — Demo Script (10 Minutes)

Dealers are busy.

Your demo should be extremely simple.

---

## Step 1

Show the **customer service request page**

Example:

Customer enters:

• VIN
• issue description
• photos

---

## Step 2

Show the **dealer dashboard**

Queue example:

| Request         | Category     | Photos |
| --------------- | ------------ | ------ |
| Slide stuck     | Slide system | 3      |
| AC blowing warm | HVAC         | 1      |

---

## Step 3

Explain the value

> “Instead of answering service calls all day, customers can submit requests here and your technicians see the issue before the RV arrives.”

---

# Step 5 — Convert Design Partners

Once the product works for them:

Offer early adopter pricing.

Example:

$99–$199/month for the first year.

You can raise prices later.

---

# Step 6 — Use Them as Case Studies

After a few weeks:

Ask them:

• Did phone calls decrease?
• Did technicians get better information?
• Did it help scheduling?

Use that feedback in sales.

Example message:

> One dealership using this reduced service intake calls by about 30%.

That makes future sales easier.

---

# Step 7 — Expand to 20 Dealers

Once you have 5–10 happy dealers, sales becomes much easier.

Your pitch becomes:

> “Several dealerships are already using this to manage service requests.”

Social proof matters a lot.

---

# Why This Strategy Works

Many founders try to build a product first and sell later.

This approach flips it:

1. talk to dealers
2. build exactly what they need
3. launch with customers already waiting

It dramatically increases success probability.
