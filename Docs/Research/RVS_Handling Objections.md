If you sell software to RV dealerships, you will almost certainly hear **the same three objections** in the first few minutes of a conversation. Preparing for these ahead of time dramatically increases your chance of early sales and hitting your **$50K ARR goal**.

Below are the **three most common objections** and how to design the product so they mostly disappear. 🚐

---

# Objection 1

## “Our DMS already does that.”

Dealers rely heavily on systems like **Lightspeed DMS**, **IDS Astra G2**, and **EverLogic**. Because the DMS touches inventory, accounting, and service work orders, dealers assume it should handle everything.

### What they actually mean

They’re worried about:

• replacing existing software
• duplicating work
• training staff on another system

Even if their DMS **does not solve the intake problem well**, they will still say this reflexively.

---

## Design the product so this objection disappears

Position the product as **“before the DMS.”**

Your tool should only handle the **customer request stage**, not work orders.

Workflow:

Customer submits request → Advisor reviews → Advisor creates work order in DMS.

Make it clear:

> “This doesn’t replace your DMS. It just collects better service information before you create the work order.”

---

## Feature that helps eliminate the objection

**One-click work order summary**

When advisors review a request they can copy a structured summary into the DMS:

Example:

```
Customer: John Smith
VIN: 1ABC234...
Issue: Slide will not retract fully
Attachments: 3 photos
Notes: Grinding noise during extension
```

This makes the system feel like a **helper**, not a replacement.

---

# Objection 2

## “Our customers will just call anyway.”

Dealers assume customers will always prefer calling.

This is partly true for older owners.

But younger RV buyers increasingly prefer digital communication. The RV owner demographic has been shifting younger over the past decade according to **Recreation Vehicle Industry Association**.

---

## Design the product so this objection disappears

Your system must be **extremely simple for customers**.

Think:

• no account required
• mobile friendly
• 60-second submission
• ability to upload photos immediately

The experience should feel like sending a message, not filling out paperwork.

---

## Feature that helps eliminate the objection

**QR code for service requests**

Dealers can place a QR code:

• on their website
• at the service counter
• in email signatures

Customers scan → submit request instantly.

This converts phone calls into structured requests.

---

# Objection 3

## “We don’t have time to implement new software.”

Dealerships are busy environments.

Anything that requires:

• integrations
• training sessions
• IT setup

will face resistance.

---

## Design the product so this objection disappears

The system must be **plug-and-play**.

Dealer setup should take **less than 10 minutes**.

Steps:

1. Create dealer account
2. Add service email address
3. Place service request link on website

Done.

No integrations required initially.

---

## Feature that helps eliminate the objection

**Dealer onboarding wizard**

When a dealer signs up, guide them through:

• uploading logo
• generating service request link
• copying embed code

This removes friction.

---

# The Hidden Fourth Objection

(This is the one most founders miss.)

## “This sounds nice, but why should I pay for it?”

Dealers will always compare your product to “just answering the phone.”

You must connect it to **real operational benefits**.

Example:

If service advisors answer **40 service calls per day** and each call averages **3 minutes**, that is **2 hours per day spent on intake calls**.

Even reducing calls by **25%** saves significant staff time.

---

# How to Frame the Value

Your pitch should be extremely direct:

> “This helps RV dealers reduce service phone calls and collect better repair information before the RV arrives.”

That message resonates immediately.

---

# The Most Important Product Design Principle

Your first version should feel like a **simple utility**, not enterprise software.

If a dealer can understand the value in **30 seconds**, your sales process becomes dramatically easier.

---

✅ **Summary**

To sell quickly, design the product so dealers feel that it:

• does not replace their DMS
• is easy for customers to use
• requires almost no setup
• clearly reduces service phone calls

If those four conditions are met, the product becomes much easier to sell.
